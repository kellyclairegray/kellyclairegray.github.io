Take a quiz about infamous women in stem!

What woman won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903 for her contribution to the understanding of radiation?

Incorrect. Try another answer!

Correct! Marie Curie won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903 for her contribution to the understanding of radiation. She was also the first person to win two Nobel Prizes, and the only person to win in multiple sciences. Those prizes were the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903, and the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911.

Who has been nicknamed “the mother of the internet”?

Incorrect. Try another answer!

Correct! Radia Perlman is a computer programmer and network engineer most famous for her creation of the spanning-tree protocol, which was a contribution that led to our modern-day conception of the internet.

Who is the only woman to have received, by herself (unshared), a Nobel Prize for Medicine?

Incorrect. Try another answer!

Correct! Barbara McClintock won the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1983 for the discovery of genetic transposition.

The first computer programmer was a woman.

Incorrect. Try another answer!

Correct! About a century before Konrad Zuse designed the first programmable computing machine, in the 1840s, Ada Lovelace wrote the first computer program in the world.

Who was the first woman to go to space?

Incorrect. Try another answer!

Correct! On June 16, 1963, Valentina Tereshkova was launched in the spacecraft Vostok 6, which completed 48 orbits in 71 hours.

Who invented signal hopping, which was a precursor to wi-fi, GPS, and bluetooth?

Incorrect. Try another answer!

Correct! Not only did Hedy Lamar invent signal hopping, but she was also a well-known film actress.

Who is known as the inventor of the first compiler?

Incorrect. Try another answer!

Correct! Grace Hopper is an American computer scientist and US Navy rear admiral known for inventing the compiler in addition to her other contributions to computer science.
